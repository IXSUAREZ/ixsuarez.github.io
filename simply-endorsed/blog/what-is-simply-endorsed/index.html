What Is Simply Endorsed?

Diego Suarez
· April 23, 2026 · ~5 min read

Simply Endorsed is a free, searchable reference tool that covers every FAA endorsement in AC 61-65K — the advisory circular CFIs use when signing a student's logbook. Instead of scrolling through a dense PDF, you type a keyword, FAR citation, endorsement ID, or checkride use case and get the exact endorsement language in seconds.

The tool lives at /simply-endorsed/ and requires no login, no download, and no account. It is built specifically for the endorsement language CFIs need on the flight line and the endorsement context student pilots want to understand before their checkride.

Who it's for

CFIs

Certified Flight Instructors

Look up the exact endorsement ID and wording before signing a logbook. Search by the student's task, FAR citation, or common alias like "solo" or "flight review."

Students

Student Pilots

Understand what endorsements you need at each stage of training — solo, cross-country, written test, checkride — and why your instructor is signing off on each one.

Schools & DPEs

Flight Schools & Examiners

A quick cross-reference during pre-checkride briefings or standardization meetings — no need to open the full AC 61-65 PDF when you just need one endorsement.

What it covers

Every endorsement in AC 61-65K, organized into the categories pilots and instructors look up most.

A.1–A.14

Student pilot & solo flight

Pre-solo knowledge, pre-solo flight training, solo authorization, solo cross-country, and the TSA citizenship endorsement method.

A.32–A.44

Checkride preparation

Written test eligibility, practical test recommendations, and knowledge-test deficiency sign-offs for private, instrument, commercial, and CFI applicants.

A.68–A.70

Flight review & currency

The standard flight review (BFR) endorsement, the WINGS-program alternative, and the instrument proficiency check sign-off.

A.71–A.77

Aircraft-specific endorsements

Tailwheel, complex, high-performance, high-altitude, pressurized aircraft, and light-sport-specific endorsements.

How to use Simply Endorsed

1

Search by keyword, ID, or FAR

Type anything into the search box — "solo," "A.6," "61.87," "flight review," or "tailwheel" — and the tool filters to matching endorsements instantly. No submit button needed.

2

Browse by certificate or workflow

Use the sidebar filters to narrow by certificate level (student, private, commercial, instrument, CFI) or by common checkride workflows. Useful when you want to see all endorsements for a given path at once.

3

Read the full endorsement text

Each result shows the endorsement ID, the AC 61-65K section reference, common aliases, the FAR citation, and the full endorsement language. Expand any card for the complete context.

Why Simply Endorsed exists

AC 61-65K is a 60-page advisory circular that most CFIs bookmark but rarely read cover to cover. When you are on the flight line and need to confirm whether A.3 or A.4 covers pre-solo training, or whether the flight review endorsement is A.69 or A.70, the PDF is slow to navigate and easy to misread under time pressure.

Simply Endorsed was built to close that gap — a fast, searchable layer on top of the full circular that returns the right endorsement language without the friction of a PDF reader. The tool does not replace AC 61-65K, your CFI certificate, or your own judgment. It is a reference aid, the same way a POH checklist supports airmanship rather than replacing it.

Frequently asked questions

Is Simply Endorsed free?
Yes, completely free. No account, no subscription, no download. Open the tool and start searching immediately.
Does it replace the official AC 61-65K circular?
No. Simply Endorsed is a reference aid, not an official FAA document. Always verify endorsement language against the current AC 61-65 before signing a logbook. The full circular is available on the FAA website.
Who can use it?
Anyone in flight training — CFIs, student pilots, sport pilots, ground instructors, DPEs, and flight school staff. The tool is designed as a quick-reference layer, useful any time you need to look up an endorsement without opening the full PDF.
Does it cover all endorsements in AC 61-65K?
Yes. Simply Endorsed includes every endorsement in the AC 61-65K library, organized with their IDs, FAR citations, aliases, and full endorsement language.
Can I search by FAR citation, like 14 CFR 61.87?
Yes. The search bar accepts FAR citations (61.87, 14 CFR 61.31, etc.), endorsement IDs (A.3, A.69), common aliases (BFR, solo, tailwheel), and plain-language keywords. Results filter as you type.
