Why A.44 is the key endorsement in this path

The instrument-rating path uses a combined endorsement that many pilots and instructors remember only vaguely until checkride month. A.42 is the written-prep endorsement for the instrument knowledge test. A.43 is the practical-test recommendation tied to the instrument training requirements. A.44 then combines the recent-prep and written-deficiency review logic for the instrument practical test in one endorsement that is valid for 2 calendar months.

That combination is exactly why the Simply Endorsed instrument bundle is useful. It shows the practical-test recommendation, the combined prerequisite endorsement, and the surrounding written-prep context in one place so the instrument applicant does not get treated like a generic add-on to a different checkride path.

Common instrument checkride endorsements

A.42

Aeronautical knowledge test

Shows the applicant completed the required ground training or review and is ready for the instrument rating knowledge test.

14 CFR §§ 61.35(a)(1) and 61.65(a) and (b)

A.43

Flight proficiency/practical test

Shows the applicant completed the required training and is proficient for the instrument practical test.

14 CFR § 61.65(a)(6)

A.44

Prerequisites for instrument practical tests

Combines the instrument practical-test recommendation with review of knowledge-test deficiencies and stays valid for 2 calendar months.

14 CFR § 61.39(a)

A.1

Common supporting reference

The app also surfaces A.1 as a common supporting reference because instructors often still think in terms of the general recent-prep endorsement pattern.

FAQ: instrument checkride endorsements

Why is A.44 different from the standard A.1 and A.2 pattern?
Because A.44 is the instrument-specific combined prerequisite endorsement. It rolls recent practical-test preparation and review of written-test deficiencies into one instrument practical-test signoff.
Is A.42 the written-test endorsement for instrument rating?
Yes. A.42 is the aeronautical knowledge-test endorsement used for the instrument rating knowledge test path.
How long is A.44 valid?
A.44 is tied to a 2-calendar-month validity window, which is one reason instructors review instrument paperwork again close to the practical-test date.
Does this page replace the actual 61.65 and 61.39 eligibility review?
No. This guide is a reference summary of the common endorsement bundle. Instructors still need to verify the applicant's actual aeronautical experience, training, and eligibility before recommending the checkride.
