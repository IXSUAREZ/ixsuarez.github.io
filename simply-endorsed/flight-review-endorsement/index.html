Simply Endorsed Flight Review Endorsement Guide

Flight review questions are common because pilots often search for the informal term “BFR” even though the actual regulatory path lives under 14 CFR 61.56. In Simply Endorsed, the flight-review-and-WINGS section puts the core flight review signoff and the WINGS substitution path together so recurrent proficiency workflows are easier to understand.

That matters because a pilot looking for a basic flight review endorsement is not usually searching for the whole AC 61-65 document. The Simply Endorsed view keeps those recurrent items organized without forcing the pilot or instructor to guess where the flight review language lives in the advisory circular.

What lives in the flight review path

The recurrent section of AC 61-65K is not only about flight reviews, but the flight-review-and-WINGS subcategory is the fastest place to start when the task is 14 CFR 61.56 compliance. A.69 is the completion of a flight review endorsement. A.70 documents completion of a qualifying WINGS phase that can substitute for the flight review requirement under the FAA-sponsored Pilot Proficiency Program.

Simply Endorsed keeps those items together because they answer closely related questions: did the pilot complete a traditional flight review, or did the pilot complete a WINGS phase that satisfies the requirement another way? That bundle view is especially useful when pilots ask for a “BFR endorsement” even though the regulation and the endorsement text are framed around the flight review itself.

Common flight review and WINGS endorsements

A.69

Completion of a flight review

Shows a flight review is complete and the instructor found the pilot able to safely use certificate privileges.

14 CFR § 61.56(a) and (c)

A.70

Completion of any phase of an FAA-sponsored Pilot Proficiency Program (WINGS)

Documents completion of a qualifying WINGS phase that can substitute for a flight review.

14 CFR § 61.56(e)

What this guide does not include

The app separates the instrument proficiency check into its own recurrent item because an IPC is not the same thing as a standard flight review. If the pilot needs instrument currency restored, the instructor should look at the IPC endorsement path separately instead of assuming the flight review section covers that scenario automatically.

FAQ: flight review endorsements

Is a BFR the same thing as a flight review?
Yes. Pilots still say “BFR,” but the current regulatory concept is the flight review under 14 CFR 61.56, and A.69 is the completion endorsement for that path.
Can WINGS substitute for a flight review?
Yes. A qualifying WINGS phase can satisfy the flight review requirement, which is why A.70 is grouped with A.69 in the Simply Endorsed flight-review-and-WINGS section.
Does an IPC count as a flight review?
No. An IPC is a separate instrument-currency path and is treated separately in the app. Instructors should not treat the IPC endorsement as the same thing as a flight review signoff.
Does the flight review endorsement itself expire?
The endorsement documents completion of the review. Pilots still need to track the timing of their 61.56 compliance window and any other recurrent requirements that apply.
