Commercial pilot guide

Simply Endorsed Commercial Pilot Checkride Endorsements

The commercial pilot checkride path resembles the private pilot bundle in structure, but it points to a different certificate standard and a different set of training requirements. In the common commercial airplane initial checkride workflow, instructors still care about the written-prep endorsement, the review of written-test deficiencies, recent practical-test preparation, and the commercial pilot practical-test recommendation itself.

Simply Endorsed keeps that commercial path grouped in one bundle so instructors do not have to mentally swap back and forth between the private and commercial sections of AC 61-65K. The commercial bundle is built around A.39, with A.38, A.2, and A.1 as the commonly included supporting endorsements. That lets an instructor audit the recommendation path quickly before a commercial applicant steps into the oral and flight portions of the test.

Why the commercial path needs its own bundle

Commercial applicants are not just repeating the private pilot endorsement sequence with a new label. The commercial certificate points to different aeronautical knowledge and flight proficiency requirements, and the instructor's recommendation needs to reflect that. A.38 is the commercial knowledge-test endorsement, A.39 is the commercial practical-test endorsement, and the same A.2 and A.1 logic still matters when reviewing written-test deficiencies and verifying recent checkride preparation under 14 CFR 61.39.

That is why a dedicated commercial bundle is valuable. It keeps the logic of the recommendation path intact while still showing the endorsements students and instructors commonly use together at the end of training.

Common commercial pilot checkride endorsements

A.38

Aeronautical knowledge test

Shows the applicant completed the required ground training or review and is ready for the commercial pilot knowledge test.

14 CFR §§ 61.35(a)(1), 61.123(c), and 61.125

A.2

Review of deficiencies identified on airman knowledge test

Shows the instructor reviewed the missed knowledge-test areas listed on the Airman Knowledge Test Report.

14 CFR § 61.39(a)(6)(iii), as required

A.1

Prerequisites for practical test

Confirms the applicant got checkride prep within the last 2 calendar months and is ready for the practical test.

14 CFR § 61.39(a)(6)(i) and (ii)

A.39

Flight proficiency/practical test

Shows the applicant completed the required training and is proficient for the commercial pilot flight proficiency or practical test.

14 CFR §§ 61.123(e), 61.127, and 61.129

How instructors usually apply the bundle

Instructors typically use the bundle as a final recommendation checklist. The commercial applicant may have completed the written long before checkride month, so the instructor needs a quick way to confirm that the written-prep endorsement, deficiency review, and recent practical-test preparation are all aligned with the actual commercial pilot recommendation. That is the kind of last-mile workflow Simply Endorsed is designed to speed up.

FAQ: commercial pilot checkride endorsements

Is the commercial checkride bundle just the private bundle with new numbers?
No. The structure is similar, but the commercial bundle points to different aeronautical knowledge and flight proficiency requirements and uses commercial-specific endorsement text.
Does A.1 still matter for a commercial applicant?
Yes. A.1 covers recent practical-test preparation and still matters in the common commercial checkride recommendation path.
Why is A.2 still in the bundle?
A.2 documents the review of knowledge-test deficiencies shown on the Airman Knowledge Test Report. If those deficiencies existed, the instructor still needs to address them before the practical test recommendation is complete.
Can this page replace the applicant's actual checkride eligibility review?
No. This page is a reference view of the common endorsement bundle. Instructors still need to verify aeronautical experience, current training records, IACRA, and any examiner-specific workflow items.
