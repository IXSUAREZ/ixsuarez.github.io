Simply Endorsed Solo Cross-Country Endorsement Guide

The initial solo cross-country path in AC 61-65K usually hinges on two endorsements that work together. A.9 documents the student's solo cross-country training for that category, make, and model, while A.10 documents that the instructor reviewed and approved planning for a specific solo cross-country flight.

That split matters. A student can be trained and qualified for solo cross-country generally, but the instructor still needs to review the route, planning, and current conditions for the specific flight being authorized. Simply Endorsed makes that easier to understand by keeping the initial solo cross-country bundle grouped in one place instead of forcing instructors and students to reconstruct the logic from the advisory circular every time the student prepares for a route away from the home airport.

Why solo cross-country uses more than one signoff

Solo cross-country is one of the first places where student-pilot endorsements become more situational. The instructor is not only verifying that the student received the necessary training, but also that the student can plan and execute a particular solo route safely. That is why the initial solo cross-country path is not the same as first solo in the pattern and not the same as the repeated-route authorization used in narrower circumstances.

Simply Endorsed keeps A.9 and A.10 grouped because that is how instructors typically think about the job. A.9 covers the training foundation. A.10 covers the specific flight review and authorization. If a student is moving from local solo pattern work into a first solo cross-country, that distinction matters both for legal compliance and for good instructional discipline.

Endorsements in the initial solo XC path

A.9

Solo cross-country flight

Gives the student the initial solo cross-country endorsement package for that category, make, and model.

14 CFR § 61.93(c)(1) and (2)

A.10

Solo cross-country flight

Shows the instructor reviewed and approved planning for a specific solo cross-country flight. This is required before each solo XC flight.

14 CFR § 61.93(c)(3)

What this guide does not cover

This page focuses on the initial solo cross-country path represented by the `initial-solo-xc` bundle in Simply Endorsed. Repeated solo cross-country flights within 50 NM use a different endorsement path. In other words, instructors still need to verify whether the student's route fits the initial cross-country bundle or a separate repeated-route authorization before signing the logbook.

FAQ: solo cross-country endorsements

Why are there two solo cross-country endorsements in the initial path?
Because they do different jobs. A.9 documents the student's solo cross-country training and qualification, while A.10 documents that the instructor reviewed and approved planning for a specific solo cross-country flight.
Is A.10 required for each solo cross-country flight?
Yes. A.10 is the per-flight planning review and approval endorsement for the specific solo cross-country flight being authorized.
Do first-solo endorsements replace solo cross-country endorsements?
No. First-solo endorsements cover the student's initial solo authorization in that make and model. Solo cross-country adds separate training and route-approval requirements.
What if the student repeats a route within 50 NM?
That can fall under a different endorsement path. Instructors still need to verify whether the student's route belongs in the initial solo cross-country bundle or a repeated-route authorization.
