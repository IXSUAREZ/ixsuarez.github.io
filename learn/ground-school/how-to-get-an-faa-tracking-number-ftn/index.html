How to Get an FAA Tracking Number

Learn what an FAA Tracking Number is, why pilots need an FTN for IACRA, student pilot applications, and knowledge test scheduling.

Diego Suarez - Updated 2026-04-27 - 3 min read
On this page What the FTN Is For When to Get One How to Get an FTN If You Already Have a Certificate

An FAA Tracking Number, usually called an FTN, is a permanent identifier used in the FAA airman certification and testing system. If you plan to take an FAA airman knowledge test or apply for an airman certificate, you will need one.

The basic process is simple: create an applicant account in IACRA, complete the registration information, and save the FTN that the system assigns to you.

What the FTN Is For

The FTN helps the FAA connect your training, applications, knowledge tests, and certification records to the correct person.

Names can create problems. People change names, share similar names, use middle initials inconsistently, or enter information differently on different forms. The FTN gives the FAA a stable identifier that stays with you through your aviation career.

You may need your FTN for:

  • Knowledge test scheduling.
  • IACRA certificate applications, including the student pilot certificate.
  • Instructor or examiner paperwork.
  • Certification questions.
  • Testing records.

When to Get One

Get your FTN early. If you are starting flight training, planning a knowledge test, applying for a student pilot certificate, or working toward another FAA airman certificate, there is no good reason to wait.

You will need it before scheduling most FAA knowledge tests through the testing provider. It is also used in IACRA applications.

How to Get an FTN

Go to IACRA and register for an account as an applicant. Enter your legal name and personal information carefully. Your name should match your identification documents.

If you do not already hold an FAA airman certificate, you can skip certificate information that does not apply and complete the applicant registration.

After registration, IACRA will display your FTN. Save it immediately. A screenshot, password manager note, and printed training folder copy are all reasonable backups.

If You Already Have a Certificate

If you already hold an FAA airman certificate, you likely already have an FTN assigned. Log in to IACRA and look at your user information. Your FTN should appear in your profile.

If you cannot access your account, use the account recovery process rather than creating duplicate accounts.

Can You Get a New FTN?

No. The FTN is intended to stay with you. If you forget it, recover your IACRA account or contact the appropriate support channel. Do not try to create a second identity in the system.

Common Mistakes

The biggest mistake is entering your name differently across systems. Use the same legal name format on IACRA, test scheduling, certificate applications, and identification.

Another mistake is confusing the FTN with other numbers. It is not your pilot certificate number, FCC FRN, medical certificate, or aircraft registration number. The FCC number is part of a separate process for items such as an FCC Restricted Radiotelephone Operator Permit.

Finally, do not wait until test day. Handle the FTN before you are ready to schedule the written test.

Final Takeaway

The FAA Tracking Number is a small administrative step that supports the rest of your training. Get it early, store it safely, and use the same legal information across your FAA testing and certification paperwork.
